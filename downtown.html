Overview

Base loop is ~ 30 minutes round trip driving time.
Again, don't want to be misleading, you don't actually walk church st for this loop, but you're close to it.
Yes Fiddlehead is included in this loop as well, it's close enough to downtown I felt the need to include it. You can of course omit it and start at switchback, but why cut corners.

This loop goes backwards compared to the others. But I recommend starting out and working your way in. By the end you're on the waterfront in downtown at Foam brewers. So if you're staying in town you don't even need a DD by the end.

Fiddlehead

website
6305 Shelburne Rd
Shelburne, VT 05482

Hours:
Sunday-Friday 12-9pm
Saturday 11-9pm

Beer

  • Some rotating cans are availabe. Typically only Second Fiddle, and occasionaly Mastermind
  • Typically three other beers on draft. Almost always their flagship ipa

Food

  • There is no food at all through Fiddlehead, but they share a building with Folino's pizza
  • Folino's is a great place for a good wood fired pizza, and they do things right. They have no beer or alcohol themselves, but is BYOB. So fill up a growler at Fiddlehead and bring it over where they provide chilled glasses
  • If there is a non-beer person with your group, there's a great place Shelburne Vineyard across the street. So pick up a bottle of your favorite wine and bring it over to Folino's; if that's more your style

Notes

  • Just recently built out a large brewing location right behind
  • Tasting room is small, get in get out, or of course, stay for pizza

Switchback

website
15 minutes from Fiddlehead
160 Flynn Ave
Burlington, VT 05406

Hours:
Monday-Thursday 12-8pm
Friday-Saturday 11-9pm
Sunday 11-7pm

Beer

  • Always pouring their full line, and usually one or two special releases
  • Must try is their flagship Switchback Ale. Unfiltered pale ale. Never met anyone who didn't like it.
  • Bombers of just about everyting is available there as well

Food

  • Some snacky things, but don't plan on eating here

Notes

  • Tasting room isn't overly large, but overflow outside in the summer is nice
  • They do a lot of great events which are always worth paying attention to. Stretch and sip yoga is always a good recomendation.
  • Great story of two friends who decided the world needed better beer

Zero Gravity

website
2 Minutes from Switchback
716 Pine St
Burlington, VT 05401

Hours:
Wednesday-Monday 12-9pm
Closed Tuesday

Beer

  • They typically have around 10 beers on draft, which gives you a good taste of their linup
  • Solid tasting room inside, as well as great overflow outside in the summer
  • They have dozens of beers at any point in time, for more see the notes section

Food

  • Solid munchies if you need something to soak up the beer
  • If you're looking for real food, I highly recommend the location on St. Paul, see the notes section for more

Notes

  • First stop on Pint st (pine st)
  • They have two locations, one here, and the other at 115 St. Paul st in downtown Burlington
  • If you're looking for a meal, i recommend the St. Paul st location. It's paired with American Flat Bread. Delicious wood grilled pizza, and a couple dozen zero gravity beers on tap. Great outside eating as well. Look for the hummingbird painted on the side of the brick building

Queen City

website
0 Minutes from Zero Gravity (across the street)
703 Pine St
Burlington VT 05401

Hours:
Tuesday-Wednesday 12-8pm
Thursday-Saturday 12-10pm
Sunday 12-7pm

Beer

  • A lot of eurpoean roots in their brewing
  • The almost always have at least one seasonal/specialty beer they're pouring
  • Bombers of almost all their beers are for sale
  • Do a tasting flight and call it good

Food

  • no... no

Notes

  • Solid little tap room, well decorated
  • The name 'Queen City' actually comes from Burlington itself. Back in the day Queen city was the unoficial name of Burlington

Citizen Cider

website
2 Minutes from Queen City
316 Pine St #114
Burlington, VT 05401

Hours:
Monday-Saturday 11-10pm
Sunday 11-7pm

Beer

  • Probably my favorite cider place
  • Impressive number of different ciders on tap
  • They also have rotating guest taps (3 or 4) for the non-cider people
  • For the non-cider people, I would recommend you try the Lake Hopper
  • Bombers or cans of almost all their beers are for sale

Food

  • Best place for food on Pine st. If you're doing this loop, I would recommend here, or the St. Paul American Flatbread
  • Good sized menu of pub style food

Notes

  • This is a good sized tasting room/eating place. But it does fill up
  • If you're around on a busy weekend, you may not be able to stop
  • Also, their service can be slow, they typically only have 2 or so servers for all the tables, so they're usually behind. if you can sit at the bar.

Foam Brewers

website
4 Minutes from Citizen Cider
112 Lake St
Burlington, VT 05401

Hours:
Monday-Thursday 12-10pm
Friday-Saturday 11am-Midnight
Sunday 11-7pm

Beer

  • At least 8, sometimes more, drafts at any point in time
  • They do an excelent job with their ipas, but also with some of their sour/saisons, probably best to try several of both
  • They are starting to offer bottles, but I would recommend growlers for anyone wanting to take some home

Food

  • They do have some small plates. I've honestly never eaten her myself tho, so I can't really comment more than that

Notes

  • The best for last? One of my favorite places. Perfect spot down by the water. Order a beer and sit out side
  • They have plenty of parking, but if you're looking to end your day, I would recommend parking at a more permanent spot and walking down hill to it
  • Only a year old and a full line of delicous beers. This place was started by a small group of people who knew the industry
  • The head brewmaster was the head brewmaster at Magic Hat up until they were bought, did a quick stint at switchback, before helping to found this place
