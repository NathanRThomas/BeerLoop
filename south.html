A scenic drive down route 7,
through the heart of Middlebury

Overview

Base loop is ~ 1.5 hours round trip driving time.
Modified loop to include Foley Brothers ~ 2.5 hours.

Fiddlehead is right in Shelburne, and then there's less than a mile between Woodchuck and Otter Creek.

Fiddlehead

website
6305 Shelburne Rd
Shelburne, VT 05482

Hours:
Sunday-Friday 12-9pm
Saturday 11-9pm

Beer

  • Some rotating cans are available. Typically Second Fiddle, Hodad, and occasionally Mastermind
  • Typically three other beers on draft. Almost always their flagship ipa

Food

  • There is no food at all through Fiddlehead, but they share a building with Folino's pizza
  • Folino's is a great place for a good wood fired pizza, and they do things right. They have no beer or alcohol themselves, but is BYOB. So fill up a growler at Fiddlehead and bring it over where they provide chilled glasses
  • If there is a non-beer person with your group, there's a great place Shelburne Vineyard across the street. So pick up a bottle of your favorite wine and bring it over to Folino's; if that's more your style

Notes

  • Just recently built out a large brewing location right behind
  • Tasting room is small, get in get out, or of course, stay for pizza

Woodchuck

website
30 minutes from Fiddlehead
1321 Exchange St
Middlebury, VT 05753

Hours:
Wednesday-Friday 11-6pm
Saturday-Sunday 11-5pm

Beer

  • Yes, this is cider and not beer, but it's still worth the stop
  • 20 draft lines
  • For non-cider people: try a seasonal or the semi-dry

Food

  • They have some stuff, mostly snacks last time I was there. You're probably not going to do a full meal there

Notes

  • Down the street from Otter Creek
  • Good sized tasting room
  • They have events from time to time, so checkout their page

Otter Creek / Shed

website
1 Minute from Woodchuck
793 Exchange St
Middlebury, VT 05753

Hours:
7 Days 11-6pm

Beer

  • Good number of lines, and they usually do a good job of limited/special editions on draft
  • Essentially this would also be the Shed brewery, so they have several lines of Shed beer as well
  • Anyone who hasn't tried the Shed IPA in a while needs to try it again. Re-done with a close throwback to Wolavers IPA from back in the day

Food

  • Simple stuff, but they do have warm food if you're looking for a quick bite, or your by yourself

Notes

  • Down the street from Woodchuck
  • Decent sized tasting room
  • They also own/are part of Shed brewing. Back in the day Otter Creek and Shed were owned by Wolavers. Since then, Wolavers is no more, and there is a third-party that's technically the owner of both Otter and Shed. But don't hold that against them, they still make good beer

  • Small place for sure, but a full line of delicious beers

Food

  • no... no

Notes

  • This is on the modified loop, it's an extra 25 minutes each way, but I promise you won't be disappointed
  • Not a big place, but well done
