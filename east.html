A quick lap through Stowe

Overview

Base loop is ~ 2.5 hours round trip driving time.
Modified loop to include Hill Farmstead ~ 4 hours.

I don't mean to mislead anyone. You don't actually see Stowe Mountain on this loop. If you're interested tho, you can get there in like 10 minutes from The Alchemist.

Also, as a note, there's a few places on this loop that are closed on Sundays, so plan accordingly

Prohibition Pig

website
23 S Main St
Waterbury, VT 05676

Hours:
Tuesday-Thursday 4-11pm
Saturday-Monday 11:30-11pm

Beer

  • This is a full brewery, and have a full line of their own beers on tap
  • They also have rotating guest lines as well
  • The beer is good, but really stop here for food to begin your tour

Food

  • One of the best spots for food of any brewery in VT. Full pub fair at a decent price
  • They have another brewery out back, behind the main building, on Elm st. They serve a nice lunch/pub fair there as well

Notes

  • This address is for the "main" place. They have a brewery section behind this building as well
  • This is the "infamous" location where The Alchemist used to be back in the days before hurricane Irene
  • If you're looking for food and good beer, the Reservoir is right next door, although not itself a brewery, great food and beer list

The Alchemist

website
20 minutes from Prohibition Pig
100 Cottage Club Rd
Stowe, VT 05672

Hours:
Tuesday-Saturday 11-7pm
Closed Sunday

Beer

  • Yes, this is the place with Heady Topper but that's not all they have.
  • They'll typically be pouring 3 draft lines: Heady Topper Focal Banger and usually The Crusher, although sometimes it will rotate
  • They have a pretty high limit on their cans, you won't go thirsty
  • Their website is well updated, and does a good job of letting you know what's available and what's sold out

Food

  • nope

Notes

  • This is their flagship store. Built just a few years ago. It essentially makes everything except for Topper, which is still made at the old place in Waterbury (nothing for the public there tho)
  • There is a small tasting room, but this isn't going to be a place to hang out. Get your checkin, buy your cans, and move on to the next place

Von Trapp

website
4 minutes from The Alchemist
1333 Luce Hill Rd
Stowe, VT 05672

Hours:
7 Days 11:30-9pm

Beer

  • Hands down the best Austrian/German style lagers in the states
  • Pretty full line as far as lagers are concerned, nothing darker then their Trosten dark lager tho. No baltic porters here

Food

  • Some fun food items. Very Austrian in style. Good for a quick lunch bite, or at least a pretzel

Notes

  • Yes this is the family from the Sound of Music
  • Bierhall is relatively new and quite beautiful
  • Great views outside as well
  • Close to the rest of the Von Trapp "stuff", hotel and horses if you're interested in a drive-by tour

Rock Art

website
20 minutes from Von Trapp
632 Laporte Rd
Morristown, VT 05661

Hours:
Monday-Saturday 10-6pm
Closed Sunday

Beer

  • 8 or so taps, offering tastings only, but hey, that's what you're looking for
  • They'll fill growlers with most of their beers as well
  • The store carries most of their beers in either cans or bottles

Food

  • Not the last time I was there no.

Notes

  • Solid tasting room. Not big, but not usually that busy either
  • They have a store as part of the tasting room, good spot for unusual bottles and merch.
  • Ask the bartender if they have Heady Topper there, they love that question. (ok, don't really ask)

Lost Nation

website
3 minutes from Rock Art
B, 87 Old Creamery Rd
Morristown, VT 05661

Hours:
Wednesday-Sunday 11:30-9pm

Beer

  • Usually a good list of their beers on draft at any time
  • They do have a limited number of different cans and bottles for sale as well

Food

  • Very limited menu, but the daily specials are good

Notes

  • Small tasting room. Can get crowded. Go in the summer so you can pour over outside
  • A little awkward to find, it's back there, look for the sign
  • Looks and feels like what you'd expect a VT brewery to be like, very authentic

Hill Farmstead

website
40 minutes from Lost Nation
403 Hill Rd
Greensboro Bend, VT 05842

Hours:
Wednesday-Saturday 12-5pm
Closed Sunday

Beer

  • Impressive draft list, probably 12 beers at any point in time
  • They also have "Onsite" only bottles. This is the good stuff, rare beers that they limit so you can only consume them there, no take homes. And for some of the higher percent darker beers, they make you drink it with a buddy
  • The have probably an overly generous growler policy, you can fill as many as you want
  • Always check out the retail shop, they have bottles you can take home with you
  • As a note, most beers are a little sour to the taste, has to do with the wild yeast that Shaun Hill likes to use

Food

  • Nothing warm that I've seen. Nuts, cheese, and fruit plates kinda thing
  • Eat before you go

Notes

  • This is on the modified loop, it's an extra 40 minutes each way, but I promise you won't be disappointed
  • Really can't say enough about the quality of the beers at this place. They've won best brewer in the world according to RateBeer.com the last 3 consecutive years, and 4 out of the last 5
  • Honestly one of the most beautiful breweries you'll see. Yes they do weddings there
  • Really Really Really in the middle of nowhere. Remeber how you got there, cause your gps won't work right away leaving
  • Be aware of when you're going, they're very slow, and lines can get long if you go on long weekends
