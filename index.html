Nate's Beer Loops!

A guide to casual drinking in the Burlington area

In order to have a successful beer drinking trip,
you need a plan

I've spent a while drinking in the Burlington area. Vermont has some of the best breweries in the world. And if you believe RateBeer.com, the best in the world, Hill Farmstead. This is by no means a comprehensive list, and new breweries are popping up all the time. There are several places that aren't on these loops *cough* Magic Hat *cough*. I simply created loops of breweries I myself stop at, or would recommend to others.

The loop times are just the driving time, starting "somewhere" in the Burlington area, out to all the stops, and back. Add in additional time for drinking/eating at the places. I always recommend a cooler with some ice before starting on the trip. Not only for keeping cold beer cold, but it blocks the sunlight as well.

I started this website, one cause this is what I do for a living, but also it's always the most effective way to share information with others. I always have friends and family asking where they should go, and where they should eat. So, here's my opinion. If anyone else out there finds this at all useful... well... I'll drink to that.
